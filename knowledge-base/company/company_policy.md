## Code of Conduct

### Professional Behavior
All employees are expected to maintain the highest standards of professional conduct. This includes:
- Treating colleagues, clients, and partners with respect and dignity
- Maintaining confidentiality of sensitive information
- Acting with integrity in all business dealings
- Complying with all applicable laws and regulations

### Harassment and Discrimination
The company has a zero-tolerance policy for harassment, discrimination, or any form of inappropriate behavior. All employees have the right to work in an environment free from harassment, discrimination, and retaliation.

### Conflicts of Interest
Employees must avoid situations where personal interests conflict with company interests. Any potential conflicts must be disclosed to management immediately.

## Workplace Policies

### Office Hours
Standard office hours are Monday through Friday, 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM. Flexible working arrangements may be available based on role requirements and manager approval.

### Dress Code
The company maintains a business casual dress code. Employees should dress appropriately for their role and client interactions.

### Use of Company Resources
Company resources, including equipment, software, and facilities, are provided for business use. Limited personal use is permitted but must not interfere with work responsibilities.

### Visitors and Guests
All visitors must be registered at reception and accompanied by an employee while on company premises.

---

## Remote Work Policy

### Eligibility
Remote work arrangements are available for eligible positions. Eligibility is determined by job function, performance history, and manager approval.

### Requirements
Remote employees must:
- Maintain a dedicated, professional workspace
- Ensure reliable internet connectivity
- Be available during core business hours
- Attend mandatory in-person meetings as required

### Equipment
The company will provide necessary equipment for remote work. Employees are responsible for maintaining company equipment in good condition.

### Communication
Remote employees must maintain regular communication with their team and manager through approved communication channels.

## Leave and Time Off

### Vacation Time
Full-time employees accrue vacation time based on years of service:
- 0-2 years: 15 days per year
- 3-5 years: 20 days per year
- 6+ years: 25 days per year

Vacation requests must be submitted at least two weeks in advance and require manager approval.

### Sick Leave
Employees are entitled to 10 paid sick days per year. Sick leave may be used for personal illness or medical appointments.

### Personal Days
Each employee receives 3 personal days per year for personal matters that cannot be scheduled outside of work hours.

### Holidays
The company observes the following holidays:
- New Year's Day
- Memorial Day
- Independence Day
- Labor Day
- Thanksgiving Day
- Day after Thanksgiving
- Christmas Day

### Family and Medical Leave
Eligible employees may take up to 12 weeks of unpaid leave per year for qualifying family or medical reasons under the Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA).

## Compensation and Benefits

### Salary
Salaries are reviewed annually during the performance review cycle. Adjustments are based on performance, market conditions, and company financial position.

### Health Insurance
The company offers comprehensive health insurance plans, including medical, dental, and vision coverage. The company contributes 80% of the premium cost.

### Retirement Plan
Employees are eligible to participate in the company's 401(k) plan after 90 days of employment. The company matches employee contributions up to 5% of salary.

### Life Insurance
Basic life insurance coverage is provided at no cost to employees. Additional coverage may be purchased.

### Professional Development
The company encourages professional growth and provides:
- Annual training budget per employee
- Conference attendance opportunities
- Educational reimbursement programs

## Performance Management

### Performance Reviews
Annual performance reviews are conducted to assess employee performance, set goals, and discuss career development.

### Goal Setting
Employees work with their managers to set annual goals aligned with company objectives. Goals are reviewed quarterly.

### Feedback
Regular feedback is provided through:
- Quarterly check-ins with managers
- 360-degree feedback processes
- Peer reviews

### Performance Improvement
Employees not meeting performance expectations will be placed on a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) with clear objectives and timelines.

## IT and Security Policies

### Acceptable Use
Employees must use company IT resources responsibly and in accordance with all policies. Prohibited activities include:
- Accessing inappropriate websites
- Installing unauthorized software
- Sharing login credentials
- Violating copyright or intellectual property rights

### Data Security
All employees must:
- Use strong, unique passwords
- Enable multi-factor authentication where available
- Report security incidents immediately
- Follow data classification and handling procedures

### Bring Your Own Device (BYOD)
Employees using personal devices for work must comply with company security policies and install required security software.

### Email and Communication
Company email and communication systems are for business use. Employees should:
- Use professional language
- Not send confidential information without encryption
- Follow retention policies

## Health and Safety

### Workplace Safety
The company is committed to providing a safe work environment. Employees must:
- Report unsafe conditions immediately
- Follow all safety procedures
- Participate in safety training
- Use personal protective equipment when required

### Emergency Procedures
All employees must be familiar with emergency evacuation procedures and assembly points. Emergency contact information must be kept up to date.

### Workplace Injuries
Any workplace injury must be reported immediately to a supervisor and Human Resources.

## Equal Opportunity and Diversity

### Equal Employment Opportunity
The company is an equal opportunity employer. We do not discriminate on the basis of race, color, religion, sex, national origin, age, disability, sexual orientation, gender identity, or any other protected characteristic.

### Diversity and Inclusion
We are committed to fostering a diverse and inclusive workplace where all employees feel valued and can contribute to their full potential.

### Accommodations
The company provides reasonable accommodations for employees with disabilities to enable them to perform essential job functions.

## Disciplinary Procedures

### Progressive Discipline
The company follows a progressive discipline approach:
1. Verbal warning
2. Written warning
3. Final written warning
4. Suspension or termination

### Immediate Termination
Certain violations may result in immediate termination, including:
- Theft or fraud
- Violence or threats of violence
- Gross misconduct
- Violation of confidentiality agreements
- Illegal activities

### Appeal Process
Employees may appeal disciplinary actions through the Human Resources department.
