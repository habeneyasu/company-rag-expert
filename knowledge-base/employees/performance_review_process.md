# Performance Review Process

## Overview

The performance review process is designed to support employee growth, align individual goals with company objectives, and provide regular feedback for continuous improvement.

## Review Cycles

### Annual Performance Review
- **Timing**: Conducted in Q1 for the previous calendar year
- **Duration**: 2-3 weeks from start to completion
- **Participants**: Employee, direct manager, and HR business partner

### Quarterly Check-ins
- **Timing**: End of each quarter
- **Format**: Informal 1-on-1 discussion
- **Focus**: Goal progress, feedback, and support needs

### Mid-Year Review
- **Timing**: Mid-year (June/July)
- **Format**: Formal review similar to annual but shorter
- **Focus**: Progress assessment and goal adjustments

## Performance Review Components

### 1. Self-Assessment
Employees complete a self-assessment covering:
- Accomplishments and achievements
- Goals met and progress made
- Challenges faced and lessons learned
- Areas for development
- Career aspirations

**Timeline**: Due 1 week before review meeting

### 2. Manager Assessment
Managers provide:
- Performance evaluation against goals
- Strengths and achievements
- Areas for improvement
- Development recommendations
- Overall performance rating

**Timeline**: Completed before review meeting

### 3. 360-Degree Feedback (Optional)
For certain roles, feedback is collected from:
- Peers
- Cross-functional partners
- Direct reports (for managers)
- Internal customers

**Timeline**: Collected 2 weeks before review meeting

### 4. Review Meeting
Structured discussion covering:
- Review of assessments
- Goal achievement discussion
- Performance feedback
- Development planning
- Goal setting for next period
- Compensation discussion (if applicable)

**Duration**: 60-90 minutes

## Performance Ratings

### Rating Scale
1. **Exceeds Expectations** (5)
   - Consistently exceeds all expectations
   - Demonstrates exceptional performance
   - Makes significant contributions beyond role

2. **Meets Expectations** (4)
   - Meets all expectations consistently
   - Performs well in all areas
   - Reliable and dependable

3. **Partially Meets Expectations** (3)
   - Meets most expectations
   - Some areas need improvement
   - Generally satisfactory performance

4. **Below Expectations** (2)
   - Does not meet key expectations
   - Significant improvement needed
   - May require Performance Improvement Plan

5. **Does Not Meet Expectations** (1)
   - Fails to meet basic expectations
   - Immediate improvement required
   - Performance Improvement Plan mandatory

## Goal Setting

### SMART Goals
Goals should be:
- **Specific**: Clear and well-defined
- **Measurable**: Quantifiable outcomes
- **Achievable**: Realistic and attainable
- **Relevant**: Aligned with company and team objectives
- **Time-bound**: With clear deadlines

### Goal Categories
1. **Business Goals**: Direct impact on business outcomes
2. **Development Goals**: Skills and capabilities to develop
3. **Stretch Goals**: Ambitious goals that push boundaries

### Goal Review
- Goals reviewed quarterly
- Adjustments made as needed
- Progress tracked throughout the year

## Development Planning

### Individual Development Plan (IDP)
Each employee creates an IDP covering:
- Career aspirations
- Skills to develop
- Learning activities
- Timeline and milestones
- Support needed

### Development Opportunities
- Training courses and certifications
- Conference attendance
- Mentorship programs
- Stretch assignments
- Cross-functional projects
- Internal mobility

## Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

### When PIP is Used
- Performance consistently below expectations
- Failure to meet critical goals
- Behavioral or conduct issues
- After multiple coaching conversations

### PIP Process
1. **Identification**: Manager identifies performance gaps
2. **Documentation**: Specific issues and expectations documented
3. **Plan Creation**: Clear improvement plan with milestones
4. **Implementation**: Regular check-ins and support
5. **Evaluation**: Assessment of progress and outcomes

### PIP Duration
- Typically 30-90 days
- Regular weekly check-ins
- Clear success criteria
- Support and resources provided

## Review Documentation

### Required Documents
- Self-assessment form
- Manager evaluation form
- Goal achievement summary
- Development plan
- Review meeting notes
- Signed acknowledgment

### Documentation Standards
- Clear and specific feedback
- Examples and evidence
- Actionable recommendations
- Professional language
- Confidential and secure storage

## Best Practices

### For Employees
- Be honest and self-reflective
- Provide specific examples
- Seek clarification on feedback
- Actively participate in development planning
- Follow through on commitments

### For Managers
- Provide timely and regular feedback
- Be specific and constructive
- Focus on behavior and results
- Support employee development
- Document performance accurately

## Appeals Process

### When to Appeal
Employees may appeal performance reviews if they believe:
- The review is inaccurate or unfair
- Important information was not considered
- The process was not followed correctly

### Appeal Process
1. Discuss concerns with manager
2. Escalate to department head if unresolved
3. Contact HR business partner
4. Formal appeal submission (if needed)
5. Review by HR and leadership

## Timeline

### Annual Review Timeline
- **Week 1**: Review process announced
- **Week 2**: Self-assessments due
- **Week 3**: Manager assessments completed
- **Week 4**: Review meetings conducted
- **Week 5**: Documentation finalized and submitted

### Quarterly Check-ins
- **Week 1**: Reminder sent
- **Week 2**: Check-ins completed
- **Week 3**: Summary shared with HR
