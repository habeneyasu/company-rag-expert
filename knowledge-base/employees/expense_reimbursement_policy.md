# Expense Reimbursement Policy

## Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for business expense reimbursement to ensure fair, consistent, and compliant handling of employee expenses.

## Eligible Expenses

### Travel Expenses
- Airfare (economy class, unless approved otherwise)
- Hotel accommodations (reasonable business-class hotels)
- Ground transportation (taxis, rideshare, rental cars)
- Meals during business travel
- Parking and tolls
- Baggage fees (when necessary for business)

### Business Meals
- Client entertainment meals
- Team building meals
- Business development meals
- Conference and event meals
- Reasonable limits apply (see below)

### Professional Development
- Conference registration fees
- Training course fees
- Professional certification fees
- Educational materials and books
- Requires pre-approval for amounts over $500

### Office Supplies and Equipment
- Office supplies (when not available through company)
- Software licenses (with IT approval)
- Equipment purchases (requires pre-approval)
- Business-related subscriptions

### Communication Expenses
- Business phone calls
- Internet access during travel
- Mobile hotspot charges (when necessary)

## Non-Eligible Expenses

The following expenses are NOT eligible for reimbursement:
- Personal expenses
- Commuting costs to regular workplace
- Personal entertainment
- Alcoholic beverages (except in approved business contexts)
- Fines and penalties
- Personal insurance
- Family member travel (unless pre-approved)
- Expenses exceeding policy limits without approval

## Expense Limits

### Meal Limits (per person, per meal)
- **Breakfast**: $25
- **Lunch**: $40
- **Dinner**: $75
- **Client Entertainment**: $100 (with manager approval)

### Hotel Limits
- **Domestic**: $200 per night
- **International**: $300 per night
- Exceptions require VP-level approval

### Airfare
- Economy class standard
- Business class requires VP-level approval
- Book at least 14 days in advance when possible

## Approval Requirements

### Pre-Approval Required For
- Expenses over $1,000
- International travel
- Conference attendance over $500
- Equipment purchases
- Client entertainment over $500
- Any exception to policy limits

### Approval Authority
- **Up to $500**: Manager approval
- **$500 - $2,000**: Director approval
- **$2,000 - $5,000**: VP approval
- **Over $5,000**: C-level approval

## Expense Submission Process

### Timeline
- Submit expenses within 30 days of incurring the expense
- Expenses over 60 days old require special approval
- Expenses over 90 days old will not be reimbursed

### Required Documentation
1. **Receipts**: Original receipts for all expenses over $25
2. **Expense Report**: Complete expense report form
3. **Business Purpose**: Clear explanation of business purpose
4. **Attendees**: Names and titles for business meals
5. **Approval**: Manager approval signature/email

### Submission Methods
- **Online**: Submit through expense management system
- **Email**: For urgent or special circumstances
- **Paper**: Only when online system unavailable

## Receipt Requirements

### Acceptable Receipts
- Original itemized receipts
- Credit card statements (with itemized receipts)
- Electronic receipts (PDF or image)
- Must show: date, vendor, amount, items purchased

### Missing Receipts
- Expenses under $25: May be reimbursed without receipt
- Expenses over $25: Require receipt or explanation
- Lost receipts: Submit explanation and credit card statement

## Reimbursement Timeline

### Processing Time
- **Submission to Approval**: 3-5 business days
- **Approval to Payment**: 5-7 business days
- **Total Time**: Typically 10-12 business days

### Payment Method
- Direct deposit to bank account (preferred)
- Check payment (if direct deposit not set up)
- Payment appears in next payroll cycle

## Travel Policy Details

### Booking Travel
- Use company-approved travel agency when available
- Book through company travel portal when possible
- Compare prices and book most economical option
- Consider environmental impact of travel choices

### Per Diem (Alternative to Receipts)
- Available for extended travel (5+ days)
- Daily allowance: $75 domestic, $100 international
- Covers meals and incidental expenses
- Must be pre-approved

### Travel Insurance
- Company provides business travel insurance
- Personal travel insurance is employee responsibility
- Review coverage before international travel

## Special Circumstances

### Emergency Expenses
- Emergency expenses may be approved retroactively
- Document emergency circumstances
- Notify manager as soon as possible

### Group Expenses
- One person may submit for group
- Clearly list all attendees
- Split expenses appropriately

### Currency Exchange
- Use exchange rate on date of expense
- Include exchange rate documentation
- Convert to company base currency

## Compliance and Auditing

### Audit Process
- Random audits of expense reports
- Review for policy compliance
- Verification of receipts and documentation

### Policy Violations
- First violation: Warning and education
- Repeated violations: Disciplinary action
- Fraudulent expenses: Immediate termination

### Record Keeping
- Expense records retained for 7 years
- Available for tax and audit purposes
- Maintained in compliance with regulations

## Tax Implications

### Taxable Expenses
- Personal portions of business expenses
- Spousal travel (unless business-related)
- Excessive or unreasonable expenses

### Non-Taxable Expenses
- Legitimate business expenses
- Properly documented expenses
- Within policy limits

### Year-End Reporting
- Form 1099 may be issued for certain reimbursements
- Consult with HR or tax advisor for questions
